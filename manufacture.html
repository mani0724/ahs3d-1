Case Study

Aerospace Industry – Inspection of Composite Materials

Introduction :

In the aerospace industry, composite materials are widely used due to their high strength-to-weight ratio. The manufacturing process involves selecting appropriate materials, preparing and curing them, and performing final inspections and testing.

The Problem Statement:

An aerospace company needed to inspect the internal structure of composite materials used in aircraft components for defects such as delaminations, voids, and fiber misalignment

Solutions:

The company utilized CT analysis to perform non-destructive testing of the composite materials. The CT scans provided high-resolution, 3D images of the internal structure, allowing for detailed inspection of potential defects.

Impact:

The implementation of CT analysis for non-destructive testing significantly improved the detection of internal defects in composite materials, which were previously undetectable through conventional inspection methods.

This advancement led to enhanced quality control, superior material performance, and greater safety and reliability of the aircraft components. As a result, the company was able to ensure higher standards of safety and performance in their aerospace products, ultimately contributing to increased customer trust and satisfaction.
